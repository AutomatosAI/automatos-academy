## 🎬 Video (~7 min) — NotebookLM → Video → Customize
```
Audience: a beginner who just wants someone to tell them "use this one." No technical background.
Explain plainly first, then name the term. Cover ONLY: how to actually choose between OpenAI's GPT,
Anthropic's Claude, Google's Gemini and the rest — WITHOUT pretending there's one winner. The honest
core, said out loud: there is no single "best" model; they're all excellent and they leapfrog each
other every few months, so "best" changes and depends on the job. What actually decides it for a
beginner: (1) the JOB — long careful writing and analysis vs quick chat vs coding vs images; some
lean one way today but all are strong; (2) COST and speed for how much you'll use it; (3) where your
DATA can go (privacy/region rules); (4) what you already have access to. The grown-up move isn't
picking a favourite — it's staying free to switch as they improve and as your job changes. Analogy:
asking "which is the best vehicle" — pointless without knowing the journey; a van, a motorbike and a
saloon each win a different trip. ~7 minutes: open with "everyone wants me to name the best — I'm not
going to, and here's why that's good news," the four deciding factors, the 2–3 mistakes (loyalty to
one brand; chasing every new 'best' headline; assuming the priciest is right for simple jobs), close
with "Match the model to the job, and keep the freedom to change. That freedom is the real skill."
Ground strictly in the sources; name real products, crown no winner, no invented benchmarks.
```

## 🎧 Deep Dive (learn) — NotebookLM → Audio → Customize
```
Two hosts, warm and plain, for a beginner who wants a straight answer. Give the honest one: no
single best model — they're all strong, they overtake each other constantly, and the right pick is a
function of the job, cost/speed, data/privacy needs, and access. Teach the beginner to reason by
task instead of by brand loyalty, and to treat model choice as reversible. Walk one or two everyday
scenarios (a careful legal-ish summary vs a fast casual reply) and show how the sensible pick can
differ — without asserting fixed winners, since capabilities move. Name the vehicles-for-journeys
analogy. The liberating takeaway: the pros don't marry a model; they stay portable. Ground strictly
in the sources.
```

## 🤝 The Automatos peek
This is the whole reason a platform like Automatos lets you use any model with your own key — because
"which is best" has no permanent answer. You can run a cheap fast model for routine work and a
frontier one for the hard calls, in the same workspace, and swap as the leaderboard changes. The
honest answer to "which should I use?" turns out to be "keep the freedom to use any of them."
