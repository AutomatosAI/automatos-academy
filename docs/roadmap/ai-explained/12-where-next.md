# AIX · Module 12 — You get it now — where next?

**Type:** Skills track (no exam) · Lane: Foundations · Kills: *"OK, I finally understand AI. Now what do I do with it?"*

**No new theory.** This module recaps the vocabulary the learner now owns and routes them to the
right next door. It's the completion of AIX and the hand-off into the rest of the academy.

## 🎬 Video (~6 min) — NotebookLM → Video → Customize
```
Audience: a beginner who just finished AI Explained — they came in confused and now know the words.
Warm, celebratory-but-plain, zero hype. Cover ONLY: a fast recap of what they now understand, and
where to go next. Recap each idea in one line — you know what AI actually is (a well-read new hire),
what an agent is (that hire with a job, knowledge and tools), text vs images vs video (same trick, different materials), why there are so many models and how
to choose (by the job, staying free to switch), open vs closed (rent vs own), the knobs (mostly
leave them; temperature sometimes), context (the desk — bigger holds more, wiped each chat), memory (the notebook beside the desk), how AI
learns your stuff (give it your documents — RAG), knowledge graphs (the map of how your things
connect), and MCP (USB-C for AI tools). Then the two doors: if you want to USE this in your business,
the next step is "AI Business Foundations" — plain, hands-on, you'll ship one real automation; if you
want to go PRO and get certified, there's a whole practitioner lane; and if you'd like a first
recognised credential without going deep-technical, Google's Generative AI Leader is a friendly one.
~6 minutes: open with "a week ago these words meant nothing to you — look how much you now understand,"
the one-line recap of all eleven ideas, then the honest signpost to the two doors, close with "You're
not an expert — you're literate. That's the hard part done. Pick your door." Ground strictly in the
sources; no pressure, no selling.
```

