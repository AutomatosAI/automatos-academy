## 🎬 Video (~7 min) — NotebookLM → Video → Customize
```
Audience: a beginner who's seen AI write essays, AI make paintings, and AI generate video clips, and
can't tell if that's one technology or three. No technical background. Explain plainly first, then
name the term. Cover ONLY: the map of what AI can read and make. The one core idea: it's the SAME
trick — learn patterns from millions of examples, then generate more of the same material — applied
to different materials. Trained on text, it writes; trained on images, it paints; trained on video,
it makes clips; same family, different diet. Two directions a beginner should separate: UNDERSTANDING
(you show it a photo or a document and it can tell you what's in it — "reading" more than text) and
MAKING (it generates new text, images, audio or video from your description). The word for an AI that
can handle several materials at once — read a photo, hear audio, answer in text — is "multimodal"
(modes = kinds of material; now you can forget the word). Honest expectations, plainly: text is the
most mature and reliable; images are strong; video is newest and improving fast; and everything from
Module 00 still applies — beautiful output, zero guarantee, so a generated image or clip still gets
a human look before it represents you. ~7 minutes: open with "one trick, different materials," the
understanding-vs-making split with one everyday example each (photograph your bookshelf and ask
what's worth reading — understanding; describe a poster and get one drawn — making), the 2–3
confusions (thinking image AI 'looks things up' — it generates from patterns; thinking one tool must
do everything — many are specialists; thinking video AI means instant movies — clips, improving),
close with "Same idea, different materials. If it learned from it, it can read it and make more of
it." Ground strictly in the sources; name no specific image/video products as 'best', no invented
capabilities.
```

## 🎧 Deep Dive (learn) — NotebookLM → Audio → Customize
```
Two hosts, warm and plain, for a beginner. Teach the modalities map: the same learn-patterns-then-
generate idea applied to text, images, audio and video; the two directions (understanding what you
show it vs making new material from your description); and "multimodal" as the word for models that
handle several materials — introduced after its plain meaning and then dropped. Set honest maturity
expectations (text most dependable, images strong, video newest) and carry Module 00's rule across:
generated pictures and clips are drafts to review, not guaranteed truth — hallucination has visual
forms too (wrong logos, impossible hands, invented details). Everyday examples throughout; no
product league tables. End with the practical takeaway: pick by the material your job needs, and
review anything generated before it represents you. Ground strictly in the sources.
```
