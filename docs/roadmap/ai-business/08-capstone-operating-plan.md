# ABF · Module 08 — Capstone: your AI operating plan

**Type:** Skills track (no exam) · Lane: Operator · Question: *"Make it real in MY business."*

**No new theory.** The capstone assembles what Modules 00–07 built into two shipped things: one
automation running end-to-end, and a one-page AI operating plan. This is the module that makes the
learner "AI-operational" — and it's the completion the badge certifies
([PRD-CREDENTIALS.md](../../prds/PRD-CREDENTIALS.md)).

## 🎬 Video (~6 min) — NotebookLM → Video → Customize
```
Audience: a smart business owner finishing the AI Business Foundations course — they've done every
module's hands-on step; this video sends them off. Advisor tone, plain English, zero hype. Cover
ONLY: assembling the pieces into an operating plan. Recap the artifact chain in one breath each —
you know what AI is and isn't (00), you mapped where it fits (01), you can brief it (02), it knows
your business (03), one workflow runs without you (04), with guardrails you chose (05), under a
policy your team has read (06), and you know what it costs and frees (07). Then the one-page
operating plan, four sections: RUNNING (the automation that's live, its owner, its budget, its
review rhythm), NEXT (the two circled candidates from the map, in order, with a start date each),
RULES (the policy page, referenced not rewritten), and REVIEW (the monthly 15 minutes: cost tab,
kill-list, one new candidate). Close with the honest send-off: "You're not 'doing AI' now — you're
running a slightly better business. The plan fits on one page because operating plans that work
always do. Review it monthly, widen the lane as trust earns it, and when a workflow outgrows this
course, the platform track (APA) and the certification lane are next doors over." Ground strictly
in the sources.
```

## 🎧 Deep Dive (send-off) — NotebookLM → Audio → Customize
```
Two hosts, advisor tone, plain English. Walk a business owner through writing and LIVING the
one-page AI operating plan: the four sections (Running / Next / Rules / Review) with a worked
example — the e-commerce shop's finished page read aloud and dissected; how the monthly 15-minute
review actually goes (cost tab, one scale/keep/kill call, promote one candidate from Next to
Running, one policy sanity-check); the failure modes of month three (novelty fades and the review
gets skipped — calendar it; the plan grows to four pages — cut it back; a new tool tempts a
restart — the plan outlives tools); and the growth paths from here, stated without selling:
deeper platform mastery (the APA track), a real business credential (the Gen-AI-Leader exam
sub-track), and the practitioner lane for anyone on the team who caught the building bug. End by
naming what "AI-operational" means: not expertise — a running system, a written policy, a review
habit, and the judgment to know what stays human. Ground strictly in the sources.
```

## 🛠 Do it now (on the free platform) — the capstone checklist
Ship it, end to end:
1. **Running:** your Module 04 automation has run ≥3 times on schedule, with Module 05's budget
   cap and approval line set, and this month's cost visible on the cost tab.
2. **The plan:** ask your grounded assistant to draft the one-pager from your own artifacts
   (map, policy, ROI sheet) — Running / Next / Rules / Review — then edit it to true.
3. **The team:** policy page shared; workspace roles match it; one person besides you can run
   and check the automation.
4. **The review:** a monthly 15-minute calendar slot exists, titled "AI review", agenda in the
   plan.
5. **Claim it:** completion badge ([PRD-CREDENTIALS](../../prds/PRD-CREDENTIALS.md)) — share it
   if it's useful to you; the plan is the real certificate.
