## 🎬 Video (~6–8 min) — NotebookLM → Video → Customize
```
Audience: a smart business owner with zero AI background and no patience for hype — explain like a
trusted advisor, plain English before any term of art. Cover ONLY: the ladder from chatting to
systems — the moment you notice you've typed the same brief for the third Friday running, you're
ready to stop chatting and start building. Three rungs, each in plain words: (1) a SAVED BRIEF —
your Module 02 delegation brief, kept and reused; (2) a PLAYBOOK — that brief turned into a recipe
with steps that runs on demand or on a schedule ("every Friday 4pm: read this week's orders, draft
the weekly summary, file it here"); (3) an AGENT — a described team member with a job, your
knowledge base from Module 03, and the tools it's allowed to use, which handles VARIED work inside
its lane rather than one fixed recipe. Worked example throughout: the café's Friday supplier-order
routine going from 40 minutes of chat-and-paste, to a saved brief, to a scheduled playbook that
drafts the order sheet, to a supplies agent that also answers "are we low on oat milk?" mid-week.
The rule of thumb: SAME steps every time → playbook; JUDGMENT within a lane → agent; and either
way a person approves the result until Module 05 says otherwise. ~7 minutes: the 2–3 mistakes
(staying in chat forever; jumping straight to a do-everything agent instead of one boring
excellent playbook; automating a process you haven't fixed), close with: "The third time you type
the same brief, don't type it — build it." Ground strictly in the sources; do not invent platform
features.
```

## 🎧 Deep Dive (learn) — NotebookLM → Audio → Customize
```
Two hosts, advisor tone, plain English before any term of art. Teach a business owner to climb
from ad-hoc chats to a small automated system: recognising automation candidates (the Module 01
map plus the felt signal of re-typing); anatomy of a playbook at owner level — trigger (on demand,
on schedule, on event like "new order email arrives"), steps (read this, draft that, file it
there), and output that lands where work actually happens; anatomy of an agent at owner level — a
role description, its knowledge (Module 03), its allowed tools, its boundaries — a hire, not a
script; choosing between them honestly (fixed steps vs judgment-in-a-lane) and why "playbook
first, agent when the playbook keeps needing exceptions" is the safe default; and keeping a human
approval on every output for now — Module 05 decides, deliberately, what earns autonomy. End with
the artifact: one real weekly task running as a scheduled playbook, human-approved. Ground
strictly in the sources.
```

## 🛠 Do it now (on the free platform)
Automate the task you circled in Module 01. Take its Module 02 brief and build it as an Automatos
playbook: set the trigger (start with a weekly schedule), the steps, and where the output lands.
Run it manually once, fix what's off, then let the schedule take it. Keep approval on the output —
you're the checkpoint until Module 05. If the task keeps needing judgment calls the playbook can't
make, that's your signal to promote it to an agent — do that only after the playbook has run twice.
