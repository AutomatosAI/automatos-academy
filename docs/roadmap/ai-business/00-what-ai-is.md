## 🎬 Video (~6–8 min) — NotebookLM → Video → Customize
```
Audience: a smart business owner with zero AI background and no patience for hype — explain like a
trusted advisor, plain English before any term of art. Cover ONLY: what today's AI actually is — a
system trained on enormous amounts of text that predicts good next words, which turns out to be
astonishingly useful for reading, writing, summarising, and deciding-with-guidance — and the four
things every owner must internalise: (1) it is a confident intern, not an oracle — brilliant output,
zero guarantee, so important facts get checked; (2) it sometimes makes things up with total
confidence — this is called hallucination, it is normal, and workflows are designed around it, not
surprised by it; (3) it only knows what it was trained on plus whatever YOU show it — it does not
know your prices, your policies, or this morning's orders unless you give them to it; (4) it is
already good enough to do real work in ordinary businesses — this is not a someday technology. Use
ONE worked example throughout: a café owner asking it to draft a supplier complaint, watching it
nail the tone, then catching it inventing a delivery date — and fixing that by pasting the actual
delivery email in. ~7 minutes: open with "you've been pitched AI ten times this month — here's what
it actually is," then the four points via the café story, then the 2–3 things owners get wrong
(thinking it's a search engine; thinking one bad answer means it's useless; thinking you need to be
technical), close with: "It's a tireless, well-read intern. Never an oracle. Manage it like one and
it will amaze you." Ground strictly in the sources; no vendor pitches, no invented statistics.
```

## 🎧 Deep Dive (learn) — NotebookLM → Audio → Customize
```
Two hosts, advisor tone, zero jargon-first language — every term of art arrives AFTER its plain
meaning. Teach a business owner what modern AI is: how a language model works at owner level (it
predicts likely next words from patterns in what it read — no database of facts, no understanding
guarantee); why that makes it superb at drafting, summarising, rewriting, extracting, and answering
from supplied material; hallucination as a designed-around property, not a scandal; the knowledge
boundary (training data vs what you paste in or upload — it knows nothing about your business until
you show it); and capability honestly stated: where it beats a person (speed, patience, breadth,
first drafts), where a person beats it (accountability, taste, current facts, the final call). End
with the mental model to carry through the whole course: capable intern, needs briefing, needs
checking, never blamed — managed. Ground strictly in the sources; do not invent benchmark numbers
or vendor claims.
```

## 🛠 Do it now (on the free platform)
Have your first *grounded* conversation. Open Automatos chat and ask something generic about your
industry — notice the answer is competent but generic. Now paste in one real thing (your menu, your
price list, a recent customer email) and ask again. That difference — generic vs grounded — is the
single most important thing you'll learn today, and the rest of this course is about making it
permanent (Module 03) and automatic (Module 04).
