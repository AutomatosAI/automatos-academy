## 🎬 Video (~6–8 min) — NotebookLM → Video → Customize
```
Audience: a smart business owner with zero AI background and no patience for hype — explain like a
trusted advisor, plain English before any term of art. Cover ONLY: how to brief an AI so the output
is actually usable — and the reframe that fixes everything: you are not "prompting", you are
DELEGATING, exactly like briefing a capable new hire on day one. The five-part brief, each shown by
upgrading the same real request (the agency asking for a client proposal): WHO it should act as
("you're our senior account manager"), WHAT exactly you want ("a one-page proposal for a café
client wanting local social marketing"), the RAW MATERIAL (paste the discovery-call notes — Module
00 taught why), the SHAPE ("three sections: goals, plan, price — friendly but direct, no
buzzwords"), and the EXAMPLE ("here's a past proposal the client loved — match its feel"). Show
the same request briefed lazily vs properly and let the difference land. Then iteration: the first
answer is a first draft from an intern — say what's wrong and ask again; two rounds beats ten
minutes of prompt-polishing. ~7 minutes: open with "the difference between AI garbage and AI gold
is the brief," the 2–3 mistakes (one-line requests then blaming the tool; not giving raw material;
accepting draft one), close with: "Brief it like a good hire: role, task, material, shape, example.
Then send it back once." Ground strictly in the sources; do not invent product features.
```

## 🎧 Deep Dive (learn) — NotebookLM → Audio → Customize
```
Two hosts, advisor tone, plain English before any term of art. Teach a business owner the working
habits that make AI reliable in daily use: the delegation brief in depth (role, task, raw material,
shape, example — why each part moves quality, told through before/after pairs on real business
tasks: quote follow-up email, review reply, weekly report); iteration as management, not failure
(critique the draft, don't restart; when to give a better example vs tighter instructions); the
verification habit scaled to stakes (glance for a tweet, line-check for a customer email, full
check plus source demand for anything with numbers, dates, or promises in it — tie back to Module
00's hallucination lesson); and building the personal delegation library — the five briefs for
your five most repeated tasks, saved and reused, which is the seed of Module 04's automation.
Close honestly: none of this needs technical skill; it needs the same clarity you'd owe a human
assistant — and most people discover their briefs were the problem all along. Ground strictly in
the sources.
```

## 🛠 Do it now (on the free platform)
Build your delegation library. Take your five most repeated writing tasks (from your Module 01
opportunity map), and in Automatos chat craft the five-part brief for each — role, task, raw
material, shape, example. Run each one, iterate once, and save the versions that produced usable
output. These five briefs are reusable assets: in Module 04 the best one becomes a playbook that
runs without you.
